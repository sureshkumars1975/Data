Automated Cash Deposit Machine — Security Requirements (CIA)

Problem

Consider an automated cash deposit machine in which users provide a card or an account number to deposit cash. Give examples of confidentiality, integrity, and availability requirements associated with the system, and, in each case, indicate the degree of importance of the requirement.

Below are practical examples of confidentiality, integrity and availability requirements for an automated cash deposit machine (ACDM). Each entry shows a short rationale and an importance rating.

Requirements (card format)

Confidentiality

Protect sensitive user and transaction data from unauthorized disclosure.

  • Protect card/account numbers and PINs — Encrypt PANs, account numbers and PINs in transit and at rest. High
  • Protect transaction metadata — Limit access to amounts, timestamps, and identifiers. Medium
  • Control access to images/CCTV — Apply RBAC and retention policies to camera/envelope images. Medium

Integrity

Ensure accurate, untampered records and system behavior.

  • Accurate deposit recording — Count validation and reconciled records; cryptographic receipts. High
  • Protect transaction logs — Use signatures/HMACs and immutable storage for logs. High
  • Trusted software/firmware — Secure boot and signed updates to prevent malicious code. High
  • Sensor tamper detection — Detect and log physical manipulation of cash sensors. Medium

Availability

Keep services usable and recoverable for customers and operators.

  • High uptime — Redundancy, monitoring, and fast recovery for deposit services. High
  • Offline queueing — Securely queue transactions when network is down and sync later. Medium
  • Receipts & dispute records — Persistent references and receipts for reconciliation. Medium
  • Physical robustness — Tamper-evident hardware, UPS, and timely maintenance. High

Controls and mitigations (examples): encryption (TLS, disk crypto), HSMs for key protection, signed firmware/secure boot, tamper-evident hardware, redundant network links and queueing, UPS/backup power, monitoring/alerts, strict access controls and retention policies.
