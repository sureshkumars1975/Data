What is Doxing?

Doxing is the act of gathering personal information (dox) about someone and making it public, often to harass or threaten them. Information may be gathered from social media, public records, or data breaches.

  • Victim: person whose information is being collected.
  • Attacker: collects and publishes the information.
  • Sources: social media, public records, leaks, and data brokers.
