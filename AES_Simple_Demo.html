What is AES? Quick Overview

AES in Simple Terms

AES = A mathematical lock that scrambles your message so badly that without the right key, no one can unscramble it.

Quick Facts About AES

Feature Details
Type Symmetric Block Cipher (same key for lock & unlock)
Block Size Always 128 bits (16 bytes)
Key Sizes 128, 192, or 256 bits (stronger = longer key)
Rounds 10 (128-bit), 12 (192-bit), 14 (256-bit)
Invented 2001 by NIST (approved by US government)
Security Unbreakable with current technology

üîÑ Complete AES Encryption Process

How AES-128 Works (10 Rounds)

Your message goes through 10 complete rounds of scrambling, each round applying 4 transformations.

Step 1: Key Expansion

What happens: Your 128-bit key is expanded into 11 different round keys

Original Key (128 bits):

2B 7E 15 16 28 AE D2 A6 AB F7 15 88 09 CF 4F 3C

Expands to 11 Round Keys (each 128 bits)

Step 2: Initial Round

AddRoundKey (with Round Key 0)

Your plaintext is XORed with the first round key

Steps 3-11: Main Rounds (9 rounds for AES-128)

Each round does:

  1. SubBytes: Substitute each byte using S-Box
  2. ShiftRows: Shift rows left by varying amounts
  3. MixColumns: Mix columns using math
  4. AddRoundKey: XOR with round key

Final round is shorter:
