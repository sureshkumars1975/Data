Problem 1.1 — Automated Cash Deposit Machine (ACDM)

Users provide a card or an account number to deposit cash. Examples of confidentiality, integrity and availability requirements are below, with importance.

Confidentiality

  • Protect card PANs, account numbers and PINs — encrypt in transit and at rest. High
  • Limit access to transaction metadata (who/when/amount) — role-based access and logging. Medium
  • Protect images/CCTV/envelope photos — retention policy and access controls. Medium

Integrity

  • Ensure accurate cash counting and recording — reconciled counts and audits. High
  • Protect transaction logs from tampering — use HMACs/signatures and immutable storage. High
  • Trusted firmware and secure boot — signed updates to prevent malicious modification. High
  • Detect physical tampering on sensors — tamper sensors and alerts. Medium

Availability

  • High uptime for deposits — redundancy, monitoring and fast recovery. High
  • Offline queueing when network fails — secure local queue and later sync. Medium
  • Receipt generation and persistent references — printable/email receipts for dispute resolution. Medium
  • Physical robustness and power backup (UPS) — protect against vandalism and power loss. High

Problem 1.2 — Payment Gateway System

A payment gateway processes a user's payment (card or account) for an item. Below are CIA requirements with importance levels.

Confidentiality

  • Protect cardholder data (PAN, CVV, cardholder name) — PCI-DSS controls, encryption in transit and at rest. High
  • Protect tokenization and customer profiles — restrict access and protect keys. High
  • Protect merchant account and settlement details — limit access to authorized systems. Medium

Integrity

  • Ensure transaction integrity from authorization to settlement — cryptographic signatures, sequence IDs. High
  • Prevent replay and tampering — nonces, timestamps, and signed payloads. High
  • Accurate billing and reconciliation — end-to-end receipts and audit trails. High
  • Protect logs and dispute records — immutability and strong access control. Medium

Availability

  • High availability for payment processing — active-active architecture and global failover. High
  • Graceful degradation and queuing — accept payments offline or queue securely when downstream is unavailable. High
  • Quick fraud-detection response and throttling — maintain service while mitigating attacks. Medium
  • Disaster recovery and business continuity — RTO/RPO defined and tested. High
