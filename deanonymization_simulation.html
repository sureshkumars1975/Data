What is De-anonymization?

De-anonymization (re-identification) is the process of matching anonymized data to real individuals by combining it with other information. Even when names are removed, unique combinations of traits can allow an attacker to identify someone.

  • Anonymized dataset: data with direct identifiers removed (e.g., name, email).
  • Auxiliary data: publicly available or leaked information that can be linked (e.g., voter records, social media).
  • Attacker: combines both datasets and finds a match to infer identity.
